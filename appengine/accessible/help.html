Accessible Blockly Music Game: Help

Accessible Blockly is a version of Blockly designed for use with screen readers. It consists of two sections: a toolbox and a workspace. You can move toolbox from the workspace, and join them to create programs.

Think of the workspace as a playground containing various groups of blocks. You can use Tab or Shift-Tab to navigate between these groups, as well as to get to the toolbox.

When you're on a block, you can press the up or down arrow keys to go to the next or previous block in the group. You can also use the right arrow key to access the block's submenus, which will allow you to modify its inputs, or do other things like copying, moving or deleting the block. You can move blocks by copying them to the clipboard and pasting them on the workspace, or by marking the destination spot and then copying or moving the block to that marked spot.

At the end of each level in the game game, you'll want to end up with a single group in the workspace that forms a program. When the program is run, it should satisfy the instructions given for that level. You'll be able to run the program, listen to the music you make, and adjust your program if needed.
